Planting Corn

Timing: Plant corn after winter when soil temperatures reach 15°C.
Spacing: Plant corn in blocks or rows, not single lines, to improve pollination. Space seeds 20-30 cm apart within rows and rows 1 m apart.
Depth: Plant seeds 3-5 cm deep.
Watering: Water thoroughly after planting and keep the soil consistently moist.

Corn Care

Watering: Corn is a thirsty plant. Provide consistent moisture, especially during silking and kernel development.
Fertilizing: Corn is a heavy feeder. Apply a balanced fertilizer when plants reach knee-high and again when tasseling begins.
Weeding: Keep the area around corn plants weed-free to prevent competition for nutrients and water.
Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for pests like corn borers, aphids, and diseases like corn smut. Use appropriate control methods if necessary.
Support: Tall corn varieties may require staking or support to prevent lodging. Harvesting Corn
Timing: Corn is ready for harvest when the silks are brown and dry, and the kernels are full and plump.
Method: Harvest corn by pulling the ear from the stalk.
Storage: Corn is best eaten fresh but can be frozen or canned for later use.

Additional Tips

Succession Planting: Plant corn in multiple batches to extend the harvest season.
Crop Rotation: Rotate corn with other crops to prevent soil-borne diseases.
Companion Planting: Consider planting beans or squash near corn for added benefits.
Remember: Corn is a wind-pollinated crop, so planting in blocks will increase pollination and yield. Consistent care and attention will reward you with a delicious harvest.

Planting Soybean

Sun: Soybeans prefer full sun.
Soil: They thrive in well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0 to 6.8. Incorporate organic matter for improved soil structure.
Tillage: Prepare the soil to a depth of 10-15 cm to ensure proper seed-to-soil contact.
Timing: Plant soybeans after the last frost when soil temperatures reach 15°C.
Spacing: Plant seeds 3-5 cm deep and 7-10 cm apart. Row spacing typically ranges from 40 to 80 cm.
Seed Treatment: Consider treating seeds with inoculant to improve nitrogen fixation.

Soybean Care

Watering: Soybeans require consistent moisture, especially during flowering and pod development.
Fertilization: While soybeans can fix their own nitrogen, additional phosphorus and potassium may be necessary based on soil tests.
Weed Control: Keep the field free of weeds, especially during the early stages of growth.
Pest and Disease Management: Monitor for common soybean pests and diseases and take appropriate control measures.
Support: Soybeans generally don't require staking, but tall varieties may benefit from support in windy conditions. Harvesting Soybeans
Timing: Harvest soybeans when the leaves turn yellow and the pods become dry and brown.
Method: Combine harvesting or handpicking can be used depending on the scale of production.

Additional Tips


Crop Rotation: Rotate soybeans with other crops to improve soil health and reduce disease build-up.
Cover Crop: Consider planting a cover crop after soybean harvest to protect the soil and add organic matter.

Planting Wheat

Sun: Wheat thrives in full sun.
Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.0 to 7.0 is ideal. Incorporate organic matter for improved soil structure.
Tillage: Prepare the soil to a depth of 15-20 cm for optimal seed-to-soil contact.
Timing: Plant winter wheat in autumn, several weeks before winter. Plant spring wheat as soon as the ground can be worked in spring.
Spacing: Broadcast or drill seeds at the recommended seeding rate for your variety.
Depth: Plant seeds 3-5 cm deep.

Wheat Care

Watering: Wheat is relatively drought-tolerant, but regular moisture is essential for germination and early growth.
Fertilization: Apply a balanced fertilizer before planting or as a top dressing during the growing season.
Weed Control: Keep the field free of weeds, especially during the early stages of growth.
Pest and Disease Management: Monitor for common wheat pests and diseases and take appropriate control measures. Harvesting Wheat
Timing: Harvest wheat when the grain is fully mature and the moisture content is suitable for storage.
Method: Combine harvesting is commonly used for large-scale production.

Additional Tips

Crop Rotation: Rotate wheat with other crops to improve soil health and reduce disease build-up.
Cover Crop: Consider planting a cover crop after wheat harvest to protect the soil and add organic matter.
